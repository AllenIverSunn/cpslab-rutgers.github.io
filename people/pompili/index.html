Dr. Dario Pompili

Associate Professor

Dr. Dario Pompili joined the faculty of the Dept. of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) at Rutgers in 2007, where he is the director of the Cyber-Physical Systems Laboratory (CPS Lab) and the site co-director of the NSF I/UCRC Center for Cloud and Autonomic Computing (CAC).
He received a PhD degree in ECE from the Georgia Institute of Technology (GaTech) in 2007, where he worked at the Broadband Wireless Networking Laboratory (BWN-Lab) directed by Prof. I. F. Akyildiz. He had previously received a ‘Laurea’ (integrated BS/MS) and Doctorate degrees in Telecommunications and Systems Engineering from the University of Rome “La Sapienza,” Italy, in 2001 and 2004, respectively.
In 2011, Dr. Pompili received the National Science Foundation (NSF) CAREER Award to design efficient communication solutions for underwater multimedia applications. In 2012, he received the Office of Naval Research (ONR) Young Investigator Program (YIP) Award, one out of 26 awarded nationwide, to develop an uncertainty-aware autonomic mobile computing framework as well as the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) Young Faculty Award (YFA), one out of 51 awarded nationwide, to enable real-time information processing based on compute-intensive models for operational neuroscience. In 2015 he was nominated Rutgers Chancellor’s Scholar.
Dr. Pompili has published more than a hundred refereed scholar publications; he has about 11,000 citations, an h-index of 42, and a i10-index of 100 (Google Scholar, Sept.’20). Since 2014 he is a Senior Member of both the IEEE Communications Society and the ACM.

Research

Dr. Pompili's CPS Lab aims at designing, developing, and implementing efficient sensing strategies to transform heterogeneous, multimodal raw data into valuable information and then into knowledge through real-time, in-situ information fusion and inference. These strategies apply to those distributed systems that need to react timely to sensor information with an effective action such as CPS, which feature a tight combination of - and coordination between - the system's computational and physical elements. The CPS Lab is active in the following research areas:

Highlights

  • Wireless Ad Hoc and Sensor Networks
  • Underwater Acoustic Communications, Vehicle Coordination, Multi-robot Formation/Steering, Task Allocation
  • Thermal Management of Datacenters, Mobile Grid Computing, Green Computing
  • Cloud Radio Access Networks (C-RANs), Cognitive Radios, Software Defined Networks
  • Traffic Engineering, Overlay Networks, Network Optimization and Control
